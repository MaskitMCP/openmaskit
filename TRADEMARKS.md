# OpenMaskit Trademark Policy

**Status:** Draft. This policy reflects the project's intent and will be
reviewed by counsel before formal trademark registration. Apply common
sense in the meantime.

## The marks

"OpenMaskit"™ (the wordmark) and the OpenMaskit logo are trademarks of
Amin Malekloo. "MaskitMCP"™ is a trademark of the same owner used as the
project organization name.

These trademarks are **not** licensed under the Apache License, Version
2.0, which governs the OpenMaskit source code. Section 6 of that license
explicitly excludes trademark grants. This policy describes what
additional uses we permit.

## What you can do without asking

- **Run, modify, fork, and redistribute** the OpenMaskit source code under
  the terms of the Apache 2.0 license.
- **Refer to the project by name** in documentation, blog posts, talks,
  comparison articles, books, and academic work — including critical
  coverage. Nominative ("referential") use is fine.
- **State compatibility or interoperability**, e.g. "compatible with
  OpenMaskit," "works with OpenMaskit," "an OpenMaskit plugin," or
  "originally forked from OpenMaskit."
- **Use the unmodified logo** to link to the official project pages
  (this repository, [maskitmcp.com](https://maskitmcp.com)).

## What requires a different approach

Forks and derivative works of OpenMaskit must be released under **a
different name and logo**. You may say your project is "based on
OpenMaskit" or "a fork of OpenMaskit" — you may not call your fork
"OpenMaskit," "OpenMaskit Plus," "OpenMaskit Enterprise," or any name a
reasonable user could confuse with the official project. The same applies
to logos: do not use the OpenMaskit logo, or a recognizable variation of
it, as the identity of a different product.

Do not use the OpenMaskit name or logo in a way that suggests official
endorsement, partnership, or affiliation when none exists. "Official"
and "endorsed by the OpenMaskit team" are reserved for use by the
project maintainers.

Do not register domain names, social media handles, package names
(PyPI, npm, Homebrew, etc.), or app-store listings that incorporate
"OpenMaskit" or "MaskitMCP" in a way likely to be confused with the
official project. If you want to provide an official-quality package
in an ecosystem we don't yet maintain, talk to us first.

Do not sell merchandise, services, or hosted offerings under the
"OpenMaskit" or "MaskitMCP" name without prior written permission.

## Modifications and quality

If you distribute a modified version of OpenMaskit, follow Apache 2.0
section 4(b): mark the files you changed. If users could mistake your
modified build for an official release, choose a different name — this
protects them from receiving software they didn't ask for under our
name.

## Questions, permissions, and reports

For permission requests, partnership inquiries, or to report misuse of
the marks, write to **legal@maskitmcp.com**.

We expect to revise this policy as the project grows and as the marks
are formally registered. Significant changes will be announced in the
repository.
